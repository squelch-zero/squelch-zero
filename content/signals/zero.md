# 0

Scientific arguments the EPA makes in its [rescission](https://www.epa.gov/regulations-emissions-vehicles-and-engines/final-rule-rescission-greenhouse-gas-endangerment) of the Greenhouse Gas Endangerment Finding.

On February 12, 2026, the EPA finalized the repeal of its 2009 determination that greenhouse gas emissions endanger public health and welfare. The agency called it ["the single largest deregulatory action in U.S. history."](https://www.wri.org/insights/endangerment-finding-repeal-explained)

The 2009 finding was built on science: years of review by EPA's own experts, the National Academies of Sciences, Engineering and Medicine, and the broader scientific community. It was the legal foundation for every federal greenhouse gas regulation — vehicle emissions standards, power plant rules, industrial reporting requirements, federal sustainability mandates.

To overturn a science-based finding, you would expect a scientific argument. [The EPA does not make one.](https://salatainstitute.harvard.edu/the-legal-reasoning-behind-the-endangerment-rescission/)

---

# 3

Legal arguments the EPA uses instead.

**First:** "Air pollution" under the Clean Air Act means only pollution causing harm through local or regional exposure — not global climate change. The statute [defines air pollutant](https://www.theregreview.org/2026/02/17/farber-epas-problematic-case-for-rescinding-its-endangerment-finding/) as "any substance emitted into or entering the air" and explicitly lists "weather, visibility, and climate" as welfare effects.

**Second:** Motor vehicles don't sufficiently "cause" or "contribute to" climate harm. U.S. vehicle emissions constitute "only 1 percent of warming." As legal scholar [Daniel Farber notes](https://www.theregreview.org/2026/02/17/farber-epas-problematic-case-for-rescinding-its-endangerment-finding/), 1 percent of U.S. annual mortality is approximately 30,000 deaths.

**Third:** Regulation would be "costly and futile." The harm exists but federal action cannot meaningfully address it.

No new data. No scientific review. No expert consultation. Three legal arguments that the Clean Air Act was never meant to apply to the climate.

# 17

Years. The endangerment finding lasted from 2009 to 2026. In that time it [underpinned](https://www.wri.org/insights/endangerment-finding-repeal-explained) every federal regulation addressing greenhouse gas emissions: vehicle standards, power plant limits, the Greenhouse Gas Reporting Program for approximately 8,000 industrial facilities, federal sustainability mandates.

The EPA simultaneously [repealed all GHG vehicle emission standards](https://www.epa.gov/regulations-emissions-vehicles-and-engines/final-rule-rescission-greenhouse-gas-endangerment). Manufacturers now have zero federal obligations for the measurement, control, or reporting of greenhouse gas emissions from any highway vehicle or engine.

The agency claims [$1.3 trillion in savings](https://www.theregreview.org/2026/02/17/farber-epas-problematic-case-for-rescinding-its-endangerment-finding/) over 28 years. Its own analysis found the rescission [costs consumers $1.5 trillion](https://www.theregreview.org/2026/02/17/farber-epas-problematic-case-for-rescinding-its-endangerment-finding/) in higher fuel expenses — vehicles no longer required to meet efficiency standards burn more gas. The claimed benefit is less than the cost.

# The insurance policy

[Lawsuits were filed within days.](https://earthjustice.org/press/2026/earthjustice-and-partners-sue-epa-for-illegal-repeal-of-climate-protections) The American Public Health Association, American Lung Association, Sierra Club, Environmental Defense Fund, Earthjustice, and others [sued in the D.C. Circuit](https://www.wbur.org/news/2026/02/19/endangerment-finding-lawsuit-trump-massachusetts-greenhouse-gases) on February 18, arguing the rescission is unlawful.

They have strong ground. EPA's legal arguments conflict with [*Massachusetts v. EPA*](https://www.theregreview.org/2026/02/17/farber-epas-problematic-case-for-rescinding-its-endangerment-finding/) — the 2007 Supreme Court ruling that explicitly recognized EPA's authority and obligation to regulate greenhouse gases. The Court considered and rejected many of the same arguments the agency now advances.

If the courts strike down the rescission, the finding could be reinstated. If they order a new scientific review, EPA would need to evaluate — on the evidence — whether greenhouse gases endanger public health.

Here is where the pieces connect.

In [April 2025](https://thehill.com/policy/energy-environment/5273217-trump-administration-national-climate-assessment-cuts/), all 400 scientists working on the next National Climate Assessment were dismissed. The congressionally mandated reports were [taken offline](https://us.cnn.com/2025/07/02/climate/national-climate-assessment-websites).

In [May 2025](https://www.ehn.org/trump-administration-fires-climate-gov-team-leaving-federal-climate-science-site-in-limbo), the entire climate.gov team was fired. NOAA's primary portal for climate data communication went dark.

In September 2025, the EPA [proposed eliminating the Greenhouse Gas Reporting Program](https://www.epa.gov/newsreleases/epa-releases-proposal-end-burdensome-costly-greenhouse-gas-reporting-program-saving-24) — the only facility-level accounting of industrial emissions in the United States. The [Clean Air Task Force called the data "irreplaceable."](https://www.catf.us/resource/understanding-value-greenhouse-gas-reporting-program-impacts-epas-proposal-roll-back-ghgrp/)

NOAA lost [27% of its staff](https://www.science.org/content/article/noaa-budget-cuts-threaten-weather-forecasts). Its Office of Atmospheric Research faced approximately 74% cuts. The agencies that produce climate science — that collect the data, run the models, write the assessments — have been systematically reduced.

The legal dodge and the infrastructure cuts are not separate stories. The rescission sidesteps the science. The cuts ensure that if courts demand new science, the capacity to produce it has been diminished.

You do not need to disprove the evidence if you have already dismantled the laboratory.

---

# What comes next

The legal challenge will take years — through the D.C. Circuit and likely to the Supreme Court. But even a legal victory may not restore what was lost.

Scientific infrastructure is not a switch. You cannot fire 400 assessment scientists, defund the data programs, eliminate the reporting requirements, and reconstitute them when a court order arrives. The [American Statistical Association warned](https://www.amstat.org/news-listing/2025/12/10/the-nation-s-data-at-risk-2025-report) in December 2025 that the destruction of federal data capacity is structural, not temporary.

The 2009 endangerment finding took years to develop. It drew on decades of accumulated data, institutional knowledge, and scientific infrastructure that no longer exists at the same capacity. Rebuilding it would take years and produce something diminished.

The effect is the same whether or not it was coordinated: not denial, but erasure. Not arguing against the science, but removing the capacity to do it. Not winning the debate, but ensuring it cannot be had.
