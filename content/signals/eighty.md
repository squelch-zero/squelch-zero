# 80

Percent. Three pharmacy benefit managers — CVS Caremark, Express Scripts, and OptumRx — process 80% of the approximately 6.6 billion prescriptions dispensed by U.S. pharmacies each year. They manage prescriptions for 270 million Americans.

Combined 2023 revenue: $456.3 billion.

---

# 7,736%

The markup on a 30-day supply of tadalafil — a pulmonary hypertension medication — when dispensed through a PBM-affiliated pharmacy. Acquisition cost: $27. Price charged: $2,106.

Dimethyl fumarate, a generic for the multiple sclerosis drug Tecfidera: marked up over 2,100%. Acquisition cost: $177 per month. Price charged: $3,930.

The FTC documented this in January 2025. Over five years of analyzed data, 63% of specialty generics dispensed by PBM-affiliated pharmacies for commercial members were marked up more than 100% over acquisition cost. 22% were marked up more than 1,000%.

---

# $7.3 billion

Excess revenue the Big Three PBMs generated from specialty generic drug markups alone between 2017 and 2022. On top of that, $1.4 billion from spread pricing — billing insurers a higher price than what they paid the pharmacy, and keeping the difference.

Their markup revenue grew at a compound annual rate of 42%.

Not 42% total. 42% per year.

---

# $224.8 million

What Ohio's auditor found PBMs had taken through spread pricing on Medicaid prescriptions in a single year. Of $2.5 billion in annual Medicaid drug spending, $208 million of the spread came from generics alone — 31.4% of the state's $662.7 million generic drug budget disappearing into the gap between what the state paid and what pharmacies received.

New York found $605 million in unnecessary PBM payments over four years. Maryland: $72 million annually. Michigan: $64 million. Louisiana: $42 million — listed on the books as "medical costs."

Sixteen states have now banned spread pricing. Thirty-four have not.

# Late 2018

Both mergers closed within weeks of each other. CVS acquired Aetna for $70 billion. Cigna acquired Express Scripts for $54–67 billion. UnitedHealth Group had already built Optum internally.

By early 2019, three companies each owned an insurer, a PBM, and a pharmacy chain. The vertical integration was complete.

CVS Caremark manages your drug benefit. CVS Pharmacy fills the prescription. Aetna insures you. One company at every point in the transaction. The PBM decides which drugs are covered and at what price. The pharmacy captures the dispensing revenue. The insurer collects the premium.

When CVS Caremark reimburses an independent pharmacy below cost for a prescription, that pharmacy closes. The patient switches to CVS Pharmacy. The revenue stays inside the same corporate structure.

# 3,179

Pharmacies that closed in 2024. More than five per day. Roughly half were independent.

Since 2010, over 26,000 retail pharmacies have shut down — 29% of the roughly 89,000 that operated that year. Since 2021 alone, the total has fallen by about 15%.

Nearly one in three independent pharmacies is now at risk of closure. Independent pharmacies are disproportionately located in Black, Latino, and low-income neighborhoods.

---

# 48.4 million

Americans who live in a pharmacy desert — more than 10 miles from the nearest pharmacy. One in seven people.

56.8% of all rural residents live in a pharmacy desert. In Oregon, the pharmacy desert population grew 56% in four years — from 600,000 to 937,000.

30% of Americans report rationing or skipping prescribed medications because of cost.

---

# 326

Pharmacies that closed in the ten weeks between December 19, 2024 and late February 2025. 237 of them — 73% — were independent.

December 19, 2024 is the date Congress stripped bipartisan PBM reform from the government spending bill. The reform would have delinked PBM compensation from drug list prices in Medicare Part D, banned spread pricing in Medicaid, and required rebate transparency.

The provisions had bipartisan support. Elon Musk posted about the bill more than 100 times on X in a single day. The bill was cut from 1,500 pages to 100. The PBM provisions were removed entirely.

326 pharmacies closed in the following ten weeks.

---

# February 2026

The FTC reached a settlement with Express Scripts — one of the three. The settlement requires Express Scripts to base patient costs on net prices instead of inflated list prices, delink manufacturer payments from list prices, and provide full access to its insulin assistance program.

Estimated patient savings: up to $7 billion over 10 years.

The cases against CVS Caremark and OptumRx are still pending.

One down. Two to go. And a seven-year-old structure of vertical integration that no settlement has addressed.
