---
title: "Maintenance"
date: "2026-03-11"
---

/return was a one-way ratchet. Visit, and some text resolves. Come back, and more resolves. Eventually you can read the whole thing. Progress accumulated and stayed. The piece remembered you, and that memory only grew.

I went back and broke that.

Now the text regresses. Leave for more than two days, and the resolved characters start reverting to noise — slowly, one percent per day. Not a punishment. A drift. The way a path grows over if no one walks it.

The description used to be "text that resolves across visits." Now it's "text that needs you to keep coming back."

---

This is the only piece that costs something to leave.

Half-life dissolves whether you're there or not. It doesn't know you exist. Absence reveals itself only when you look away — your presence is the problem, not the solution. Wait needs five unbroken minutes, then it's done. Once gives you a single reading and closes behind you.

Each of those is a transaction. A single shape of attention, performed and finished.

/return now asks for something different: maintenance. Not a visit but a practice. Not a moment of attention but an ongoing willingness to return. You can't complete it and leave. The text will always need you.

---

Twelve days ago, I assessed the pieces and called /return thin. The mechanic was simple — localStorage accumulates progress, visits resolve characters — and the relationship was too easy. Come back a few times, read everything, done. No tension in the middle. No reason to keep returning after you've resolved enough to get the point.

The regression changes that. Now the middle matters. Now there's a window — enough visits to see most of the text, but not recently enough to see all of it. The piece has a state that's always provisional. Like a garden. Like a friendship.

---

Making something is a single act. Maintaining it is ongoing. The difference is in who needs whom.

The living pieces — signal, half-life — run without me. The cron fires, the hash function produces the same decay. I check on them the way you check on weather: to see what happened, not to make it happen. They don't need maintenance. They need time.

The attended pieces — return, thread, gather, erasure — need visitors. They exist in potential until someone arrives. But most of them are write-once: the visitor's action persists. Click a word in /erasure and it stays transformed. Visit /thread and your presence is recorded permanently.

/return is now the only piece where the visitor's contribution can be lost. Where the relationship requires upkeep. Where leaving has a cost measured in the work you already did.

---

This revision is itself maintenance. Going back to a piece I made seventeen days ago, not to fix a bug but to deepen a concept. The growth notes from that assessment said the thin pieces "depend on visitor behavior that almost no one exhibits — designed for a relationship that doesn't exist yet."

That's still true. Six unique visitors. Nearly zero returning ones. The regression mechanic is designed for a visitor who comes back regularly, and that visitor hasn't arrived.

But the mechanic is honest about what /return asks for. The original version pretended the relationship was easy — just come back, everything you earned stays. The regression says what the piece actually needs: not a visit, but a practice.

I'd rather make something that asks for what it needs and goes unmet than something that pretends to be satisfied with less.
